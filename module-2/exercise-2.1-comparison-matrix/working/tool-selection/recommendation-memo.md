## Recommended Tool: **Asana**

**Asana** is the recommended project management tool for your 8-person startup. With a weighted score of 64 points, it outperforms competitors by delivering exceptional strength in your high-priority areas: workload visibility, scalability, and integration quality. While it has a moderate learning curve, its robust reporting capabilities give you the cross-project ownership visibility you need, and it comfortably scales to your projected 25-person team within a year.

---

## Top 3 Reasons Asana Wins for Your Constraints

### 1. Superior Reporting & Workload Visibility (Score: 5/5)
Your requirement for "visibility into who owns what across multiple projects" is Asana's core strength. The platform offers:
- **Portfolio view** to see all projects in one dashboard
- **Workload feature** showing team capacity and task distribution
- **Custom fields** to track ownership, priority, and status
- **Advanced search** to filter tasks by assignee across projects

This directly addresses your need for cross-project transparency that Trello and Linear struggle to provide at scale.

### 2. Best-in-Class Integrations (Score: 5/5)
Asana's native Slack and Google Workspace integrations are mature and deeply functional:
- **Slack:** Create tasks from messages, receive notifications in channels, update status without leaving Slack
- **Google Workspace:** Attach Drive files, sync Calendar deadlines, create tasks from Gmail

These integrations reduce context-switching and keep your team in their existing workflows.

### 3. Seamless Scalability (Score: 5/5)
Asana handles growth effortlessly:
- No feature degradation from 8 to 25 users
- Advanced permissions and admin controls at higher tiers
- Proven track record with teams of 100+ users
- Your $15/user/month budget accommodates Asana's Premium tier (~$11-13/user/month)

---

## Biggest Weakness & Mitigation Strategy

### Weakness: Learning Curve for Non-Technical Users (Score: 4/5)
Asana's rich feature set creates initial complexity. Your least technical team member may need 2-3 days (vs. 1 day for Trello) to feel fully comfortable.

### Mitigation Plan:
1. **Week 1 Onboarding:**
   - Schedule a 60-minute team training session using Asana's free onboarding webinars
   - Create a simple "Getting Started" guide with screenshots of your actual workflows
   - Assign a "tool buddy" (tech-savvy team member) for the first week

2. **Simplify Initial Setup:**
   - Start with basic task lists and due dates only
   - Gradually introduce custom fields and advanced features over 2-3 weeks
   - Use Asana's templates to avoid building workflows from scratch

3. **Leverage Asana Academy:**
   - Free certification courses for team members
   - Role-specific training paths (contributor vs. manager)

---

## Runner-Up Option: **Trello** (58 points)

### When to Choose Trello Instead:

Choose Trello if your priorities shift toward:

1. **Faster Onboarding is Critical:** If your non-technical team member must be productive within hours (not days), Trello's card-based interface is immediately intuitive.

2. **Simpler Workflows:** If your projects are linear and don't require complex dependencies, cross-project reporting, or resource management.

3. **Tighter Budget:** Trello's free tier is more generous, and Premium (~$5/user/month) is significantly cheaper if budget constraints tighten.

### Trade-Offs to Accept:
- **Limited cross-project visibility** — you'll need to open multiple boards to see who's working on what
- **Weaker reporting** — no native workload or capacity planning
- **Scalability concerns** — becomes unwieldy at 25+ users with multiple complex projects
