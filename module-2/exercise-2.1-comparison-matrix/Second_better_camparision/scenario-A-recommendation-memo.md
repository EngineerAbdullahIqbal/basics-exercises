## Executive Summary

After comparing Trello, Asana, Notion, and Linear across price, ease of use, integrations, and mobile quality, **Asana Premium ($10.99/user/month)** is the recommended choice for our 8-person startup.

---

## Decision Rationale

### Why Asana?

1. **Best fit for mixed tech-savviness** - Our team has both technical and non-technical members. Asana strikes the right balance with a moderate learning curve (3-5 days onboarding) that won't leave anyone behind.

2. **Within budget** - At $10.99/user/month, it's comfortably under our $15 limit while offering premium features. Total cost: ~$88/month for 8 users.

3. **Excellent integrations** - Native Slack and Google Workspace integrations work seamlessly with our existing tools.

4. **Superior visibility** - Workload view and task assignment features directly address our need to see "who's working on what."

5. **Scalability** - As we grow from 8 to 25+ people, Asana handles that growth without requiring a platform migration.

### Why Not the Others?

| Tool | Key Limitation |
|------|----------------|
| **Trello** | Too basic for growing team; limited reporting without paid add-ons |
| **Notion** | Steep learning curve (1-2 weeks); non-technical team members will struggle |
| **Linear** | Built for software teams; limited Google Workspace integration; dev-focused UI |
