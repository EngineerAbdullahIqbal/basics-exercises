# Project Management Tool Comparison: Trello vs Asana vs Notion vs Linear

## Executive Summary

**Recommendation: Asana** is the best fit for your team of 8 with mixed technical skills.

### Why Asana Wins:
- ✅ **Within budget**: $10.99/user/month (Starter plan)
- ✅ **Excellent for mixed tech-savviness**: Intuitive for non-technical, powerful for technical users
- ✅ **Best visibility**: Clear task assignments, timelines, and workload views
- ✅ **Strong integrations**: Slack + Google Workspace work seamlessly
- ✅ **Scales well**: Grows smoothly from 8 to 25+ users
- ✅ **Superior reporting**: Built-in dashboards without extra cost

---

## Detailed Comparison

### 1. Trello
**Best for**: Simple, visual task management

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Price** | Free tier available; Standard: $5/user/mo; Premium: $10/user/mo |
| **Free Tier** | Unlimited cards, but only 10 boards per workspace; limited power-ups |
| **Learning Curve** | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Easiest to learn (drag-and-drop kanban) |
| **Mobile App** | Good, intuitive |
| **Integrations** | Slack ✅, Google Drive ✅, 200+ power-ups |
| **Reporting** | Basic; needs power-ups for advanced dashboards |
| **Scalability** | Good for small teams; can get messy at 25+ users |
| **Visibility** | Good via boards, but lacks advanced workload views |

**Pros:**
- Extremely easy to adopt (least technical person will learn in hours)
- Visual kanban boards are intuitive
- Affordable

**Cons:**
- Limited reporting without paid power-ups
- Can become chaotic with complex projects
- Less structured for detailed task tracking

## Final Recommendation

### 🏆 **Asana** is the best choice for your scenario.

**Reasoning:**
1. **Mixed tech-savviness**: Asana balances ease-of-use with power features. Your least technical team members can learn it quickly, while technical users get advanced capabilities.

2. **Visibility**: Asana's strength is showing who's working on what through assignee views, timelines, and workload features.

3. **Integrations**: Seamless Slack and Google Workspace integration out of the box.

4. **Budget-friendly**: At $10.99/user/month, it's well within your $15 budget.

5. **Migration timeline**: Asana offers templates and import tools that can help you migrate within 2 weeks.

### Implementation Plan (2 weeks):
- **Days 1-2**: Set up Asana workspace, create projects, invite team
- **Days 3-5**: Migrate existing tasks/projects using templates
- **Days 6-7**: Team training (focus on non-technical members)
- **Days 8-10**: Parallel run (use both old system + Asana)
- **Days 11-14**: Full migration, deactivate old system
